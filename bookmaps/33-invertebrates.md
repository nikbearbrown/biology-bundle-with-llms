## Source Structure Overview

The source (8 Markdown files, ~23,841 words) is an OpenStax Biology chapter covering invertebrate diversity from sponges (Porifera) through echinoderms, with emphasis on body plans, tissues, and life cycles.

### Pedagogical Voice
- **Register:** Expository textbook (third-person, declarative). Not narrative. Not conversational.
- **Audience:** Introductory biology students (likely sophomore level)
- **Implicit authority:** Statements presented as settled fact, not inquiry.

## Sponges Section — What Works Well

- **Specificity of mechanism:** Choanocyte structure explained mechanistically—microvilli collar, central flagellum, flagellar beating (thousands of strokes/minute), phagocytosis, intracellular digestion.
- **Functional justification:** Explains why sponges remain thin (diffusion limit) and why spongocoel architecture is efficient.
- **Developmental distinction:** Distinguishes between "functional tissues" and "true tissues" (embryologically derived).
- **Diversity within simplicity:** Catalogs sponge classes by spicule type with remarkable detail.

## Cnidarians Section — What Works Well

- **Cell-level mechanism:** Nematocyst structure and firing explained with precision. Acceleration (40,000 times gravity), time scale (microseconds), anatomy (coiled thread with barb), outcome (neurotoxins, pore-forming toxins).
- **Comparative anatomy:** Compares polyp vs. medusa forms and matches each form to its ecology.
- **Life cycle as mechanism:** Explains functional logic of *Obelia* alternation (asexual feeding polyps, sexual dispersing medusae).
- **Tissue organization:** Explicitly compares cnidarian tissue layers to sponge lack of true tissues.
