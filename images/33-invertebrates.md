### Section 2: Cnidarians and Nematocysts

**[FIGURE: Nematocyst Mechanism — Firing Sequence]**
- Four-step sequence: trigger → depressurization → eversion → toxin delivery
- Timeline in microseconds, acceleration relative to gravity
- Caption: "When a cnidocil is touched, the nematocyst capsule depressurizes. Osmotic pressure launches the coiled thread at 40,000 times gravity—faster than a rifle bullet."

**[FIGURE: Cnidarian Body Forms — Polyp vs. Medusa]**
- Side-by-side: polyp (mouth up, tentacles radiating) and medusa (mouth down, tentacles hanging)
- Both labeled with epidermis, gastrodermis, mesoglea, nerve net
- Caption: "Cnidarians express two body plans: the polyp (sessile, mouth up) and medusa (motile, mouth down)."

**[FIGURE: Obelia Life Cycle]**
- Colonial polyp → gastrozooid and gonozooid → medusa buds → male/female medusae → planula larva → settlement → new polyp → new colony
- Arrows showing cycle direction
- Caption: "The hydroid *Obelia* alternates between stationary polyps (efficient feeders) and mobile medusae (efficient dispersers). This division of labor between two forms is the life strategy."

### Section 3: Arthropods and Segmentation

**[FIGURE: Insect Segmental Organization — Grasshopper Anatomy]**
- Head (antennae, compound eyes, mandibles), thorax (three pairs of legs, two pairs of wings), abdomen (midgut, reproductive organs, spiracles)
- Ganglia chain and tracheal tubes
- Caption: "A grasshopper demonstrates the arthropod segmental template: each segment is fundamentally identical but specialized for different functions."

**[FIGURE: Exoskeleton Structure and Musculature]**
- Cross-section showing chitin cuticle, internal muscles, joint articulation
- Antagonistic muscle pairs (flexor and extensor)
- Caption: "The arthropod exoskeleton is rigid chitin that muscles pull against from inside. Joints between segments allow bending. This lever-arm system gives small muscles enormous mechanical advantage."

**[FIGURE: Arthropod Diversity — Modular Specialization]**
- Insect (beetle), crustacean (crab), arachnid (spider), centipede, millipede
- Showing variation in leg count, body region fusion, appendage specialization
- Caption: "The arthropod body plan was modified in different groups. Insects specialized for flight, crustaceans for aquatic feeding, arachnids for active predation. Same template, infinite variation."
