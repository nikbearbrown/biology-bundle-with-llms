# Image Specifications: Chapter 10 — Photosynthesis

---

## Figure 1: Chloroplast Cross-Section

**Location:** Section 2, after chloroplast structure description.

**What to show:** Outer/inner membranes, stroma, granum (stack of thylakoids), individual thylakoid with lumen, chlorophyll embedded in membrane.

**Caption:** A chloroplast contains stacked thylakoids (grouped into grana). Chlorophyll and electron transport proteins embed in the thylakoid membrane. The lumen accumulates protons during light reactions.

**Colors:** Membranes dark, stroma light blue, chlorophyll bright green, lumen lighter blue.

---

## Figure 2: Photosystem II and the Photoact

**Location:** Section 3, after "The beginning: light hits a leaf."

**What to show:** Light-harvesting complex (antenna pigments around center), reaction center with P680, incoming photon, electron transfer to primary acceptor.

**Caption:** Antenna pigments relay photon energy to the reaction center. The special chlorophyll *a* pair (P680) gives up an electron to the primary electron acceptor — the photoact. Light energy becomes an excited electron.

**Colors:** Antenna pigments varied, P680 dark green, electron shown as small sphere.

---

## Figure 3: Complete Electron Transport (PSII → PSI)

**Location:** Section 3, after "The electron flows down the chain."

**What to show:** PSII with water-splitting cluster (Mn/Ca), electron path to cytochrome complex, PSI with P700, proton pumping (arrows stroma to lumen), NADP+ reduction, oxygen release.

**Caption:** Electrons flow from PSII through the cytochrome complex to PSI. As the electron loses energy, that energy pumps protons into the lumen, building the gradient. A second photon re-energizes the electron at PSI, which then reduces NADP+ to NADPH.

**Colors:** Electron energy gradient (bright yellow to dark), protons shown as + symbols (dense in lumen, sparse in stroma), NADPH in purple.

---

## Figure 4: ATP Synthase and Chemiosmosis

**Location:** Section 3, after "Building the gradient: ATP synthesis."

**What to show:** Thylakoid membrane, proton concentration in lumen (high) vs. stroma (low), ATP synthase spanning membrane, spinning mechanism, ADP+Pi → ATP.

**Caption:** The proton gradient drives protons through ATP synthase, a spinning turbine. As protons flow, the protein spins, attaching a phosphate to ADP, forming ATP. This chemiosmosis converts gradient energy into chemical energy.

**Colors:** ATP synthase blue, ADP orange, ATP yellow, proton gradient density-coded with + symbols.

---

## Figure 5: RuBisCO and Carbon Fixation

**Location:** Section 4, after "Stage 1: Carbon Fixation."

**What to show:** RuBP (5-carbon), CO₂ approaching, RuBisCO enzyme, unstable intermediate, product (two 3-PGA). Label carbon count.

**Caption:** RuBisCO catalyzes CO₂ + RuBP → 3-PGA. The enzyme works slowly (~3 CO₂/sec) because it must distinguish CO₂ from the similar-sized O₂. That slowness is accuracy.

**Colors:** Carbons gray, phosphates red, RuBisCO as large protein shape.

---

## Figure 6: The Calvin Cycle (All Three Stages)

**Location:** Section 4, after "Stage 3: Regeneration."

**What to show:** Circular flow with three stages labeled. RuBP → (+ CO₂ via RuBisCO) → 3-PGA → (+ ATP/NADPH) → G3P; one exits, five regenerate (+ ATP) → RuBP.

**Caption:** Three Calvin cycle turns fix three CO₂ molecules. Six G3P molecules form; one is exported; five regenerate RuBP. This requires 9 ATPs and 6 NADPHs per three turns (equivalent to 3 ATP and 2 NADPH per G3P exported).

**Colors:** ATP orange, NADPH purple, energy inputs highlighted.

## Figure 7: Photon to ATP (Synthesis, Optional)

**Location:** Section 5, "Energy Flow Through the Living World."

**What to show:** Sun → photon path → chlorophyll → light reactions machinery → ATP → Calvin cycle → glucose → mitochondrion → ATP in cell → organism.

**Caption:** A photon travels 93 million miles and powers the synthesis of ATP, which fuels the Calvin cycle to build glucose. That glucose powers every mitochondrion in every organism that eats the plant.

**Colors:** Bright for high energy, gradient to darker for energy being used. Emphasize main pathway.

## Illustrator Notes

1. **Accuracy:** Consult primary sources for protein structures if detailed rendering needed.
2. **Clarity over realism:** Slightly simplified, color-coded illustration > hyperrealistic confusion.
3. **Consistent color scheme:** Chlorophyll green, photons/high energy yellow, ATP/energy-rich orange, NADPH purple, protons/+ symbols density-coded, CO₂ in one color, products in another.
4. **Scale indicators:** Micrometers for chloroplast, nanometers for molecules.
5. **Captions reference mechanism and trade-offs**, not just naming.
6. **Test each figure:** Can the mechanism be understood from image + caption without reading text?
