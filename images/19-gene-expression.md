### 1. DNA Wrapped Around Histones (after first epigenetic control paragraph)

**Placement:** Right after "Imagine a library where books (genes) are stored on shelves (chromatin)."

**Description:** Two panels side by side.
- Left: Electron microscope-style rendering of DNA wrapped around histone octamer, "beads-on-string" appearance
- Right: Labeled diagram showing histone core, DNA wrapped 1.65 turns, and measurement of ~200 bp per nucleosome

**Caption:** Histone proteins compact DNA into nucleosomes, controlling gene accessibility. Acetyl groups on histones loosen the wrap; deacetylation tightens it.

### 3. RNA Splicing (post-transcriptional section)

**Placement:** After "join exons end-to-end."

**Description:** Vertical timeline showing:
- Pre-mRNA with exons (dark) and introns (light) labeled
- Spliceosome recognition at exon-intron boundaries
- Intron removal and exon ligation
- Mature mRNA with introns removed
- All in one coordinated process

**Caption:** Spliceosomes remove introns from pre-mRNA, joining exons to create mature mRNA. Different exons can be included or excluded, producing multiple proteins from one gene.

**Source:** Can adapt from OpenStax m66507 Figure 1

### 4. The Lac Operon: Multiple States (deep-dive section)

**Placement:** Core figure in the deep-dive section

**Description:** Four horizontal panels showing:

**Panel A: Glucose present, lactose absent**
- CAP not bound to promoter
- Lac repressor bound to operator
- RNA polymerase blocked
- Label: "Transcription OFF"

**Panel B: Glucose absent, lactose absent**
- CAP-cAMP bound to promoter
- Lac repressor bound to operator
- RNA polymerase blocked (two signals must align)
- Label: "Transcription OFF"

**Panel C: Glucose present, lactose present**
- CAP not bound
- Allolactose bound to repressor; repressor released from operator
- RNA polymerase cannot access (CAP signal missing)
- Label: "Transcription LOW"

**Panel D: Glucose absent, lactose present (full induction)**
- CAP-cAMP bound to promoter, stabilizing RNA polymerase
- Allolactose bound to repressor; repressor released
- RNA polymerase actively transcribing
- mRNA being made
- Label: "Transcription HIGH"

**Caption:** The lac operon requires two signals: CAP-cAMP (glucose absent) and allolactose (lactose present). Only when both are satisfied is transcription maximal. This prevents energy waste on unnecessary enzymes.

**Source:** Adapted from OpenStax m66504 Figure 3 and supporting diagrams

### 5. Histone Modifications and Chromatin State (epigenetics section)

**Placement:** Can be integrated or standalone in epigenetics section

**Description:** Side-by-side comparison
- Left: Histone with acetyl groups (Ac) on lysine residues, DNA loosely wrapped, chromatin open ("euchromatin"), transcription factors accessible
- Right: Histone with acetyl groups removed, DNA tightly wrapped, chromatin closed ("heterochromatin"), transcription factors blocked

**Color code:** Green for acetyl groups, red for deacetylase enzyme

**Caption:** Acetylation of histones reduces positive charge, loosening DNA wrapping and opening chromatin. Deacetylation tightens wrapping and silences genes. These modifications are reversible.

**Source:** Can adapt from OpenStax m66505 Figure 3

### 6. Alternative Splicing (post-transcriptional section, optional)

**Placement:** After mention of alternative splicing generating multiple proteins

**Description:** Single pre-mRNA transcript with exons 1-7 labeled
Below: Four different mature mRNA variants showing different exon combinations:
- Variant A: 1-2-3-4-5-6-7 (full-length)
- Variant B: 1-2-4-5-6-7 (exon 3 skipped)
- Variant C: 1-2-3-6-7 (exons 4-5 skipped)
- Variant D: 1-2-5-6-7 (exons 3-4 skipped)

**Caption:** From a single gene, alternative splicing produces multiple mRNA variants. Different splice patterns are used in different tissues or developmental stages, expanding protein diversity without increasing genome size.

### 7. Five Levels of Eukaryotic Gene Regulation (optional summary visual)

**Placement:** Could appear as a summary figure or in a supplementary section

**Description:** Vertical flow diagram from nucleus to cytoplasm showing:
1. **Epigenetic level** (in nucleus): Chromatin opening/closing, histone marks
2. **Transcriptional level** (in nucleus): RNA polymerase binding, transcription factors at promoter/enhancer
3. **Post-transcriptional level** (in nucleus): Splicing, capping, polyadenylation
4. **Translational level** (in cytoplasm): Ribosome assembly, eIF-2 phosphorylation
5. **Post-translational level** (in cytoplasm): Protein modification, ubiquitination, degradation

Arrows between levels showing information flow and checkpoints.

**Caption:** Gene regulation in eukaryotes occurs at five distinct levels, from chromatin accessibility to protein degradation. Each level is reversible and responds to different signals on different time scales.

**Source:** Synthesized concept; can be custom-designed
