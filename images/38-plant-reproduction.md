# Images: Plant Reproduction

## Figure Descriptions and Placeholders

### Figure 1: Four Whorls of a Complete Flower

**Description:** Longitudinal cross-section of a complete flower showing all four whorls.

**Content:**
- Outermost whorl: green sepals (calyx)
- Second whorl: colored petals (corolla) with visible patterns
- Third whorl: stamens (androecium) with filaments and anthers
  - Anther detail box showing: bilobed structure, microsporangia inside
- Innermost whorl: carpel (gynoecium) with stigma, style, and ovary
  - Ovary detail box showing: ovules inside, integuments, micropyle opening
- Receptacle base

**Labels:**
- Sepal, Petal, Filament, Anther, Microsporangium
- Stigma, Style, Ovary, Ovule, Integument, Micropyle
- Calyx, Corolla, Perianth, Androecium, Gynoecium

**Pedagogical Purpose:** Establish flower geometry as organized for reproduction; show nested whorls and partition of reproductive functions.

### Figure 3: Embryo Sac Development

**Description:** Three-panel developmental sequence of female gametophyte.

**Content:**

**Panel A: Megasporogenesis (Meiosis)**
- Diploid megaspore mother cell
- Meiosis I and II with chromosomes shown
- Four haploid megaspores produced
- Three degenerating (grayed)
- One surviving

**Panel B: Megagametogenesis (Mitosis)**
- Surviving megaspore undergoing three rounds of mitosis
- 1-nucleus → 2-nucleus → 4-nucleus → 8-nucleus stages
- Nuclear migration and fusion shown

**Panel C: Mature Embryo Sac (8-Nucleate, 7-Cell)**
- Eight nuclei at final positions:
  - Two polar nuclei (center, fusing to form central cell) — highlighted
  - Three antipodal cells (far end, degenerate) — gray
  - Egg cell (micropyle end) — highlighted
  - Two synergid cells (flanking egg) — highlighted
- Synergid chemistry toward egg shown

**Labels:**
- Megaspore mother cell, Megaspore, Polar nuclei, Central cell
- Egg cell, Synergid cells, Antipodal cells
- Micropyle, Integument
- Megasporogenesis, Megagametogenesis

**Pedagogical Purpose:** Show female gametophyte organization for receiving sperm; establish 8-nucleate structure as setup for double fertilization.

### Figure 4: Double Fertilization

**Description:** Four-panel sequence showing pollen tube arrival and dual fertilization.

**Content:**

**Panel A: Pollen Tube Entering Ovule**
- Pollen grain with two cells (pollen tube cell, generative cell)
- Tube growing down style
- Entering ovule through micropyle
- Two sperm cells visible in tube

**Panel B: Pollen Tube in Embryo Sac**
- Tube rupturing inside sac
- Release of two sperm
- Synergids guiding pathway
- Distance marked to egg and central cell

**Panel C: First Fertilization (Egg + One Sperm)**
- Sperm approaching and fusing with egg
- Nuclei fusing in close-up
- Result: diploid zygote (2n)

**Panel D: Second Fertilization (Central Cell + Other Sperm)**
- Sperm approaching and fusing with central cell (two polar nuclei)
- Nuclei combining
- Result: triploid endosperm (3n)
- Post-fertilization: synergids degenerate, antipodal cells degenerate, micropyle closes

**Labels:**
- Pollen tube cell, Generative cell, Sperm cells
- Stigma, Style, Ovule, Micropyle
- Egg cell, Central cell, Polar nuclei, Synergid cells
- Diploid zygote (2n), Triploid endosperm (3n)

**Pedagogical Purpose:** Establish double fertilization as unique angiosperm mechanism; show how two sperm achieve different destinations.

### Figure 7: Dormancy and Germination

**Content:**

**Panel A: Light Requirement**

- Buried seed in darkness (dormant)

**Panel B: Cold Requirement**
- Seed over winter with cold exposure
- Spring warming triggers germination

**Panel C: Fire Trigger**
- Seed after forest fire
- Heat/smoke penetrating coat

**Panel D: Moisture Trigger**
- Dry seed → heavy rain
- Seed coat softening
- Radicle emergence

**Panel E: Scarification**
- Hard seed coat → mechanical/acid treatment
- Weakened coat allows water penetration

**Panel F: Seed Size and Depth**
- Large seed: deep planting (sufficient reserves)
- Small seed: surface planting (light requirement)

**Labels:**
- Dormancy cue type, Environmental condition
- Seed coat response, Metabolic changes
- Germination rate, Time scales

**Pedagogical Purpose:** Show dormancy as adaptation; demonstrate seeds "read" environment through multiple signal types.
